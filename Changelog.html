Notes

If my work looks frontloaded this milestone, see below. My presentable "products" came late, but I worked (struggled) consistently.

This milestone was the most challenging (or frustrating) for me. I worked consistently, but didn't find much to show for it. My blackjack page and game in particular were a huge struggle without much clear payoff, due to various factors. I do feel I learned a lot through my mistakes - javascript functions that were being called before they were declared and couldn't be hoisted because I had stored them in a variable, for instance. The trial and error gave me constant headaches, but if there's a silver lining, I know I won't make the same mistakes again.

Maybe the best example of this is my Bootstrap carousel and layout (and, later, these collapsible menus!). I struggled for 2 weeks on a carousel of pictures that wouldn't move, double and triple-checking my code. Everything looked correct. It was only on Thursday night that, at a glance, I noticed I was using Bootstrap v4.2., and the newest version was v5.1. I didn't think this would make a difference, but out of desperation and frustration, I revamped all my bootstrap source imports and formatting. Suddenly, everything worked perfectly. I wish I'd done that a week ago.

Ironically, in finally fixing these items, I broke others. The various tweaks and compromises I'd made to try and 'fix' my layout, which wasn't looking the way I thought it should despite my formatting, were suddenly creating a mess because they had been compensating for elements that weren't functioning properly. As I write this, I'm attempting to clean some of it up, but I apologize if the page is messy.

Final thoughts

--As I mentioned above, I am disappointed with not putting forth my intended product. I feel the project was manageable, but I both found myself very short on time going into this week, and encountered some unexpected hiccups with deployment that caused me to stumble. The etch-a-sketch isn't what I'd hoped for, but I do think it's a small, fun app that still demonstrates a degree of familiarity with the material. Even with the challenges, I feel I've learned more in this past month than in almost any other class I've taken, and despite verything that is exciting. Unfortunately I can't be proud of this portfolio, but I learned as much from my mistakes as my successes. I hope it will be a solid foundation for work I can be more proud of in the future.
